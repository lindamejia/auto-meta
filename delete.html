LGBTQIA + INCLUSIVITY 

In this project group, students will focus on the LGBTQIA+ population in Los Angeles. Students will review the current literature and best practices from other cities on the intersection between the SDGs and LGBTQIA+ populations and identify the priority SDG indicators for LGBTQIA+ disaggregation.

COMMUNITY WEALTH 

This project focuses on the socio-economic factor of “no one left behind”. A person’s ability to get out and stay out of poverty is shaped by the programs and services accessible to them. People get left behind when they lack the opportunities and capabilities to receive a quality education, earn an adequate income, accumulate wealth, or otherwise fully and equitably participate in their communities. Students in this project will analyze the effectiveness of a pilot project in the San Fernando Valley. This pilot project is focused on school-based outcomes.

COMMUNITY INDEX

The SDGs are a powerful tool for building cross-sector partnerships and connect organizations and people to launch new activities and projects. In order to effectively mobilize the community around the SDGs, it is important to have an accurate database of activities and actions related to the SDGs. Students in this project will build up the current database of SDG activities by sourcing organizations, projects, and people that are advancing the SDGs in LA.

HOUSING ACCELERATOR 

This team will work to support the launch of the SDG Housing Accelerator Program, taking action to advance the SDGs at the city level by utilizing emerging 4IR (4th Industrial Revolution) technologies to better serve individuals and families facing housing insecurity in Los Angeles and Nairobi. With support from the Hilton Foundation, this effort will bring together a dynamic, cross-sector ecosystem of local stakeholders to develop tools that assist cities, service providers, and individuals facing housing instability with predictive modeling, preventative measures, and other services that enable an adequate supply of and access to affordable housing.

STREET ASSETS 

The Los Angeles Department of Public Works - Bureau of Street Services (StreetsLA) is implementing an Asset Management framework to improve street improvement decisions by considering risk, regional mobility needs, and lifecycle consideration. As part of this effort, StreetsLA’s Advanced Planning is developing an equity-focused, multi-objective decision tool to guide the development of multi-asset projects that improve the efficiency of spending on capital projects and ongoing maintenance and operations. The tool will use existing regional and local demand, risks, asset conditions, and various funding eligibility data to improve the level of service to underserved communities.

INDOOR AIR QUALITY 

Los Angeles’ Green New Deal commits to “mandate and incentivize the transition to a zero-carbon city in a way that prioritizes the needs and opportunities of disadvantaged communities.” The City’s goal to achieve zero carbon buildings with a zero-carbon grid will have long-term benefits of reduced GHG emissions. In the near term, the City aims to ensure that the benefits of clean buildings with healthy air quality are realized in the communities that have historically suffered from disinvestment and pollution.

FEDERAL ENTITLEMENT 

This project will undertake a categorical mapping of the past seven year’s worth of projects funded by CDBG, considering 1) how those grant-funded projects align with budget allocations for similar projects across the City (e.g. how are sidewalk improvement and lighting project funds distributed across the City?); 2) whether those grant-funded projects may have been eligible for other funds (e.g., Special Fund Schedules, voter-approved projects); and 3) how similar projects in the City (not supported by CDBG) are receiving funds.
